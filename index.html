A journey from Chem-eng to AI

I began my bachelor's degree in Chemical Engineering in 2013 at Ferdowsi University of Mashhad. Upon graduating with the second-highest GPA in my group, I was accepted by the National Organization of Exceptional Talents to continue my education in an M.Sc. program without taking the university entrance exam. During my bachelor's thesis, I gained my first experience in a pharmaceutical lab, learning how to design and deliver drugs to targets using specialized carriers for efficient disease treatment. For my Master's laboratory experiments, I primarily used three photo bioreactors (PBRs) to cultivate microorganisms and micro algae (based on OD or cell count) to extract products for industrial applications. The focus of my work included: 1) Extraction of lipids and carbohydrates, essential elements for biofuels like biodiesel, at various growth stages. 2) Extraction of chlorophyll-a (Ch-a), chlorophyll-b (Ch-b), and other pigments such as carotenoids. 3) Extracting protein from unique algae with potential use as a cancer treatment. However, this journey culminated in significant contributions to the field of biotechnology, but that was an end to my Chemical engineer disciplines. I am currently a PhD candidate in artificial intelligence at Imperial College London I immersed myself in AI research, gaining invaluable experience in designing and implementing advanced machine learning algorithms for various applications. I focused on several pivotal areas within artificial intelligence:
i) Developing modern natural language processing (NLP) models to enhance text and speech understanding systems.
ii) Advancing generative adversarial networks (GANs) for realistic image and video generation.
